Introduction


Our innovative online platform is designed to turn agricultural waste into something truly valuable. Instead of letting materials like rice husks, wheat straw, sugarcane bagasse, and other agricultural byproducts go to waste, we can transform them into high-quality, strong, and durable plywood. This not only creates a eco-friendly product but also helps address two major environmental challenges: deforestation and waste management.

  Our process is both simple and impactful : By connecting farmers, suppliers, and manufacturers through a digital interface, we make it easier than ever to source, process, and produce eco-friendly plywood. Our platform ensures that valuable agricultural waste is put to good use, reducing environmental impact .We're not just creating durable plywood—we're building a greener, more eco-friendly future


Functions of Farmers

Farmers are the backbone of agriculture, responsible for cultivating crops and raising livestock to produce food, fiber, and other resources essential to society. Their work spans various types, from large-scale commercial farming to small family farms and subsistence agriculture.

Agricultural Waste

Agricultural waste is a term for the organic and inorganic materials left over after agricultural processes, such as growing crops and raising animals . Here are some of the Agricultural Waste which can be recycled.

SUBTITLE

Rice Husk

Rice husk, also known as rice hull, is the hard, protective coating on a rice grain that protects it during the growing season.

SUBTITLE

Rice Straw

Straw is an agricultural byproduct consisting of the dry stalks of cereal plants after the grain and chaff have been removed.

SUBTITLE

Sugarcane Bagasse

Sugarcane Bagasse is the dry pulpy fibrous material that remains after crushing sugarcane or sorghum stalks to extract their juice..

SUBTITLE

Coconut Shell

Coconut shell is the strongest part covered in coconut fruit. Coconut shell is located in between the coconut flesh and coconut husk.
